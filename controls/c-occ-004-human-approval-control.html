C-OCC-004

Human Approval Control

Require explicit, authority-validated human approval before any commitment above the defined thresholds is executed.

Control IDC-OCC-004
TypePreventive
Trust domainDecision · Identity
Enforcement pointApproval workflow (above threshold)
Failure postureFail-closed
Parent capabilityBox 33 · Output Commitment Control
Control objective
Ensure that material or high-impact commitments cannot be executed on agent authority alone — an appropriately authorised human must approve, with the approval captured as evidence.
Control statement
Commitments exceeding the approval thresholds (per the Commitment Threshold Matrix) must be held pending approval by a human whose delegated authority covers the action. Approval must be recorded with approver identity and rationale; absence of approval must block execution.
Design & operation
  1. Threshold evaluation (C-OCC-008) flags a commitment as requiring human approval.
  2. The commitment is held; an approval request with full context is routed to an approver whose authority is validated against the Delegated Authority Framework.
  3. The approver approves or rejects with rationale; dual authorisation is required above the highest band.
  4. On approval, execution proceeds under a state lock (C-OCC-003); on rejection or timeout, the commitment fails closed.
  5. The decision is written to the approval record (E-OCC-002).
Inputs / outputs

Inputs

  • Threshold/approval requirement (C-OCC-008)
  • Commitment context pack
  • Approver identity + delegated authority

Outputs

  • Approve / reject decision
  • Approval Record (E-OCC-002)
  • Execution release or fail-closed

Test method / assurance

Design effectiveness

  • Confirm thresholds requiring approval are defined, owned and enforced.
  • Confirm approver authority is validated, not assumed.
  • Confirm dual authorisation above the top band.

Operating effectiveness

  • Sample above-threshold commitments; confirm each has a valid E-OCC-002 from an authorised approver.
  • Test timeout path fails closed.
  • Review approval-quality / rubber-stamping indicators.
Failure modes & residual risk
  • Rubber-stamping. Approvals without genuine review; mitigated by context packs and quality review.
  • Wrong approver. Mitigated by authority validation.
  • Residual. Low with authority-validated, evidenced approval; Medium if approval quality is weak.
Regulatory mapping
  • APRA CPS 230 — human accountability for material automated actions.
  • APRA CPS 234 — segregation and approval evidence.
  • Corporations Act / ASIC — authority to bind in customer matters.
Implementation notes (vendor-neutral)
Implement via an approval workflow that validates approver entitlements in IAM and presents a complete decision context; bind approval to the state lock so approval cannot be reused against changed facts.
