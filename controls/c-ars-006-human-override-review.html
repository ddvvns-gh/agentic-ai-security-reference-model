C-ARS-006

Human Override Review

Let authorised humans override the score where it is wrong, and review overrides — so the autonomous score never has the final, unappealable word. Frontier capability.

Control IDC-ARS-006
TypePreventive · Corrective
Trust domainDecision · Identity
Enforcement pointEscalation / review workflow
Failure postureConservative
Parent capabilityBox 35 · Autonomous Risk Scoring
Control objective
Keep a human in control of a Frontier capability. Where the score is wrong (false positive blocking good work, or a missed risk), authorised humans can override, and overrides are reviewed to improve the scorer and catch abuse.
Control statement
Authorised humans must be able to override a risk-score-driven routing decision; overrides must be authority-validated, recorded and periodically reviewed for pattern and abuse.
Design & operation
  1. Frontier / emerging capability (backlog item 7). Reliable real-time scoring of agent-action risk is among the hardest things in the model and is not yet mature industry-wide. Treat the score as an input that routes for stronger controls, never as a control that may pass a high-risk action on its own confidence.
  2. Provide an override path for authorised humans (with validated authority).
  3. Record every override with rationale (E-ARS-003).
  4. Review override patterns: frequent overrides signal a poor scorer; suspicious overrides signal abuse.
  5. Feed review into scorer tuning and routing policy.
Inputs / outputs

Inputs

  • Score-driven routing decision
  • Override request + approver authority

Outputs

  • Override decision
  • Override Record (E-ARS-003)
  • Override-rate signal (M-ARS-002)

Test method / assurance

Design effectiveness

  • Confirm override requires validated authority.
  • Confirm overrides are recorded and reviewed.
  • Confirm review feeds tuning.

Operating effectiveness

  • Test an override by an authorised vs unauthorised user.
  • Measure override rate (M-ARS-002).
  • Review override patterns for scorer quality and abuse.
Failure modes & residual risk
  • Override abuse. Overrides used to bypass control; mitigated by authority validation + review.
  • Residual. Low–Medium.
Regulatory mapping
  • EU AI Act — human oversight.
  • ASD/CISA — human controllers.
  • APRA CPS 230.
Implementation notes (vendor-neutral)
Provide an authority-validated override with mandatory review; high override rates are themselves the signal that a Frontier scorer is not ready to be relied upon.
