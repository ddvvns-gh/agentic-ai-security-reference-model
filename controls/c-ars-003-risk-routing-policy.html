C-ARS-003

Risk Routing Policy

Map risk scores deterministically to routing outcomes — auto-proceed, soft evaluation (Box 37), hard response (Box 34) or escalation (Box 39) — so the (probabilistic) score drives (deterministic) action.

Control IDC-ARS-003
TypePreventive
Trust domainDecision
Enforcement pointDecision-routing gateway
Failure postureFail-closed (toward stronger control)
Parent capabilityBox 35 · Autonomous Risk Scoring
Control objective
Turn the score into governed action without trusting it as a boundary. A deterministic policy maps score bands to routing, ensuring high scores always route to stronger controls and the score never directly authorises a risky action.
Control statement
Risk scores must be mapped to routing by a deterministic, versioned policy; high scores must route to stronger controls (soft/hard/escalation) and a score alone must not auto-authorise a high-impact action.
Design & operation
  1. Frontier / emerging capability (backlog item 7). Reliable real-time scoring of agent-action risk is among the hardest things in the model and is not yet mature industry-wide. Treat the score as an input that routes for stronger controls, never as a control that may pass a high-risk action on its own confidence.
  2. Map score bands to routing via policy-as-code (auto / soft Box 37 / hard Box 34 / escalate Box 39).
  3. High-risk bands fail closed toward stronger control.
  4. Routing decisions recorded (E-ARS-002); high-risk routing coverage measured (M-ARS-001).
  5. Policy is owned and versioned with risk appetite.
Inputs / outputs

Inputs

  • Risk score + band
  • Routing policy (band → action)

Outputs

  • Deterministic routing outcome
  • Routing Decision Record (E-ARS-002)
  • High-risk routing coverage (M-ARS-001)

Test method / assurance

Design effectiveness

  • Confirm score→routing is deterministic policy-as-code.
  • Confirm high bands route to stronger controls (fail-closed).
  • Confirm a score alone cannot auto-authorise high-impact.

Operating effectiveness

  • Test boundary scores route correctly.
  • Measure high-risk routing coverage (M-ARS-001 = 100%).
  • Confirm routing recorded.
Failure modes & residual risk
  • Mis-banded score. A wrong score routes wrongly; mitigated by conservative bands + deterministic downstream controls.
  • Residual. Low–Medium (downstream controls are deterministic).
Regulatory mapping
  • ASD/CISA — graduated autonomy.
  • NIST AI RMF — Manage.
  • Risk Appetite Statement.
Implementation notes (vendor-neutral)
Implement as deterministic routing in the decision gateway; the score routes, the downstream controls (Boxes 34/37/39) enforce. This is what keeps a Frontier score safe to use.
