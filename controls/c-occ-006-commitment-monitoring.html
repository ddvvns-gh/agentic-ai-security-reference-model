C-OCC-006

Commitment Monitoring

Continuously monitor commitment activity for unusual patterns — volume spikes, threshold-edge clustering, unapproved external sends, repudiation signals — and raise exceptions.

Control IDC-OCC-006
TypeDetective
Trust domainDecision
Enforcement pointControl plane (post-decision, continuous)
Failure postureAlert
Parent capabilityBox 33 · Output Commitment Control
Control objective
Detect commitment behaviour the preventive controls did not block but that indicates abuse, circumvention or error — and feed it to risk, IR and the soft/hard response paths.
Control statement
Commitment activity must be continuously monitored against behavioural baselines and policy expectations; anomalous patterns (clustering just under thresholds, unapproved external communication, abnormal counterparties) must raise exceptions for investigation.
Design & operation
  1. Ingest commitment decisions, approvals and executions from the control plane.
  2. Evaluate against baselines and rules: just-below-threshold clustering, repeated denials, unapproved external destinations, novel counterparties.
  3. Raise exceptions into the monitoring report and route high-severity to threat detection/IR (Box 47) and decision routing.
  4. Tune rules from confirmed findings.
Inputs / outputs

Inputs

  • Commitment decision/approval/execution stream
  • Behavioural baselines + detection rules
  • Threshold context (C-OCC-008)

Outputs

  • Exceptions and trend findings
  • Monitoring Report (E-OCC-005)
  • Escalations to IR / decision routing

Test method / assurance

Design effectiveness

  • Confirm monitoring covers all commitment channels incl. external comms.
  • Confirm threshold-edge clustering detection exists.
  • Confirm escalation routing to IR.

Operating effectiveness

  • Seed a structuring pattern (splitting to stay under thresholds) in shadow; confirm detection.
  • Review exception true/false-positive rates.
  • Confirm findings feed tuning.
Failure modes & residual risk
  • Detection gap. Novel evasion not yet modelled; mitigated by tuning + dual-engine review.
  • Alert fatigue. Poorly tuned rules bury signal.
  • Residual. Medium — detective control reduces but does not prevent.
Regulatory mapping
  • APRA CPS 230 — monitoring of operational risk.
  • APRA CPS 234 — detection capability.
  • AML/CTF-style structuring analogues where financial.
Implementation notes (vendor-neutral)
Implement as continuous control monitoring over the commitment event stream (correlated via Box 46), with rules + baselining; route to SOAR/case management. Detective, not preventive — pairs with C-OCC-008.
