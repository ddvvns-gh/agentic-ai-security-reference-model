C-OCC-005

Commitment Lineage Recording

Record the full decision chain behind every commitment — intent, authority, classification, threshold, approval, execution — as connected, tamper-evident lineage.

Control IDC-OCC-005
TypeEvidentiary
Trust domainDecision
Enforcement pointControl plane (continuous)
Failure postureFail-safe (record-or-block)
Parent capabilityBox 33 · Output Commitment Control
Control objective
Make every commitment fully reconstructable after the fact: who/what initiated it, under what authority and purpose, what the controls decided, who approved, and what executed — so commitments cannot be repudiated and incidents can be explained.
Control statement
For every material commitment, a connected decision-lineage record must be produced linking intent, identity/authority, classification, threshold evaluation, approval and execution. Lineage must be tamper-evident and complete; a commitment whose lineage cannot be recorded must be blocked.
Design & operation
  1. Each commitment control emits its decision keyed by a shared commitment_id.
  2. The lineage service joins these into one chain: intent → classification → authority → threshold → approval → execution.
  3. The chain is written tamper-evident (signed/hash-linked) into the evidence store.
  4. Completeness is checked; gaps raise a lineage-incomplete exception.
  5. The chain is the source for explainability, audit and investigation.
Inputs / outputs

Inputs

  • Per-control decision records (E-OCC-001/002/007)
  • Execution log (E-OCC-003)
  • Shared commitment_id

Outputs

  • Decision Lineage Record (E-OCC-004)
  • Lineage-completeness signal (M-OCC-004)
  • Lineage-incomplete exceptions

Test method / assurance

Design effectiveness

  • Confirm every commitment control feeds lineage with a shared key.
  • Confirm tamper-evidence and completeness checking.
  • Confirm a record-failure blocks the commitment.

Operating effectiveness

  • Reconstruct a sampled commitment end-to-end from lineage.
  • Confirm M-OCC-004 = 100% for material commitments.
  • Attempt tamper in test; confirm detection.
Failure modes & residual risk
  • Broken join. A missing commitment_id fragments the chain; tracked by M-OCC-004.
  • Late evidence. Asynchronous records can momentarily show gaps; bounded by SLA.
  • Residual. Low with complete, keyed lineage.
Regulatory mapping
  • APRA CPS 230 — reconstructable accountable decisions.
  • EU AI Act — record-keeping / traceability of high-risk AI.
  • ISO 42001 / NIST AI RMF (Manage) — auditability.
Implementation notes (vendor-neutral)
Implement as a lineage/provenance service consuming control-plane events on a shared correlation key; align with the decision-lineage schema (Box 43). This control is the Box-33 instance of that enterprise discipline.
