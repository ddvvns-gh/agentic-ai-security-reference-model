One rule: enforcement intensity follows blast radius

Every action is classified on two axes — impact (its blast radius) and certainty (whether a policy decision is deterministic or requires judgement) — and a single precedence routes it to the right runtime response. This is the shared mechanism behind Output Commitment Control [33], Autonomous Risk Scoring [35] and the Decision Routing Gateway [34/36/37/39]. It exists so that the bulk of low-impact traffic stays fast and cheap, and heavyweight enforcement is reserved for where real harm is possible.

Fractal note. This matrix is the agent-tree instance of a universal rule in the Unified Entity Trust Fabric (UETF): (impact × certainty) → precedence → enforcement posture. The same rule governs every entity — human, machine, organisation — not only agents. What is correct here must be correct on every tree; that is the validation, not a limitation.
Axis 1 · Impact (blast radius)

Impact = reversibility × scope × sensitivity. It is computed from the resource and the action, floored by the charter / risk-appetite statement (a tier may be raised by the owner, never lowered below its intrinsic floor), and re-evaluated per invocation (reading one record ≠ reading the whole table).

Axis 2 · Certainty

Hard — deterministic

A clear, rule-based match: a deterministic policy is violated or satisfied with no judgement required. Routes to deterministic enforcement (Terminate / Contain).

Soft — judgement required

Probabilistic or ambiguous: behavioural drift, an evaluator's opinion, "is this within intent?". Routes to evaluation / yield — never an automatic block, to avoid false-positive denial of legitimate work.

High impact dominates certainty: a Critical action is escalated even when the rule-match is clean, because the cost of being wrong outweighs the cost of a pause. Certainty only decides enforce-vs-evaluate below the escalation line — which is what keeps detection from becoming autoimmune.

Subgraph rule: when one action activates a chain of downstream calls, its tier is the maximum tier over the whole activated subgraph — the most dangerous edge sets the posture, so a critical effect cannot be slipped through a routine-tier path.

Governance of the classification

Who sets the tier

  • Intrinsic impact is computed from the resource + action (reversibility, scope, sensitivity, data classification).
  • The charter / risk-appetite statement sets minimum floors for whole action classes (e.g. "money movement ≥ T2", "safety = T3").
  • The resource owner may raise a tier, never lower it below the floor.

Anti-downgrade

  • Operational Integrity cross-checks declared vs intrinsic tier; a mismatch (a "T0" action that touches a Critical resource) is flagged as non-self.
  • Tier is re-evaluated at exercise time for parameter-dependent actions — this is precisely why T2/T3 require fabric-side live evaluation.
