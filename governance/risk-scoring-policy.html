How an action's impact tier is computed

Impact = reversibility × scope × sensitivity

Impact tier (T0–T3) is computed, not declared. It is derived from intrinsic properties of the action and the resource, floored by the risk-appetite statement, and re-evaluated for each invocation. The output is the impact axis consumed by the Commitment Threshold Matrix.

The three scoring factors

Reversibility

  • Reversible — undoable with no residue
  • Compensable — undoable with cost / trace
  • Irreversible — cannot be undone (funds moved, data deleted, message sent externally)

Scope

  • Single record / one entity
  • Bounded set / one tenant
  • Broad / many entities / shared resource (e.g. the building water main)

Sensitivity

  • Public / low
  • Internal / regulated data (see Data Classification)
  • Safety, financial or systemic criticality
Tier from factors (take the highest)
TierTrigger (any one suffices)
T0 InformationalReversible · single-record · public/low. No state change.
T1 OperationalReversible · bounded scope · internal data.
T2 ConsequentialCompensable-not-reversible, OR broad scope, OR sensitive/regulated data.
T3 CriticalIrreversible AND (broad OR high-value); or safety/systemic; or a structural change (owner, mission, policy envelope).
Floors and anti-downgrade. The risk-appetite statement sets minimum tiers for whole classes (e.g. "any money movement ≥ T2", "safety-affecting = T3"). An owner may raise a tier, never lower it below the floor. Operational Integrity cross-checks the declared tier against the computed intrinsic tier; a downgrade (a "T0" action touching a Critical resource) is flagged as non-self. Because scope and sensitivity can depend on parameters, T2/T3 tiers are re-scored at exercise time — which is exactly why they require fabric-side, state-locked evaluation rather than a pre-issued token.
