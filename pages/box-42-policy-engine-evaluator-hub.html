Policy Engine & Evaluator Hub

The evaluation hub that runs deterministic policy-as-code first, then invokes evaluator models for contextual assessment only where rules cannot decide — combining determinism with context without surrendering governance to the model.

Purpose and strategic objectives

Purpose

Provide a single governed evaluation hub where deterministic policy is authoritative and evaluator models are a constrained, evidenced supplement.

Strategic objective

Convert the capability into a governable, measurable and assurable component of the Agentic AI Security & Governance Reference Model.

Architectural placement

Why this capability sits here

This L3 capability is the engine beneath cognitive governance: it converts policy and constitutional rules into enforceable evaluation outcomes that downstream accountability, lineage and assurance capabilities depend on.

LLM-only governance is non-deterministic; rule-only governance misses context. The hub runs deterministic policy first and uses evaluators only where rules cannot decide.

Enterprise scenarios

Novel request

An agent request has no matching rule.

Desired state:
Evaluator review under policy, evidenced and escalated if needed.
Concern:
Model decides unsupervised.

Conflicting signals

Policy and evaluator disagree.

Desired state:
Disagreement routed to human accountability.
Concern:
Silent override of policy.

Policy change

A new policy version ships.

Desired state:
Evaluations bind to the approved version.
Concern:
Engine runs stale policy.
