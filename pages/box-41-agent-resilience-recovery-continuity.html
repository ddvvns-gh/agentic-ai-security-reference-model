Ensure AI-enabled processes can fail safely and recover effectively.

This capability defines fallback, degradation, rollback, manual workarounds, recovery objectives and continuity controls for AI-enabled business processes.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To maintain operational resilience when AI agents, models, providers, data sources or controls fail.

Strategic objective

Prevent AI dependency from creating fragile business operations or unrecoverable process failures.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 4 because resilience is an operational capability that must be tested, monitored and improved over time.

If the agent fails, the business process must still have a safe path.
Problem statement

As AI agents become embedded in workflows, outages, provider failures, model regressions or control shutdowns can disrupt business processes.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Defines fallback and manual operating modes.
  • Sets recovery objectives and dependency maps.
  • Tests failover, degradation and rollback paths.
  • Maintains provider and model contingency options.
  • Captures recovery evidence.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not guarantee zero downtime.
  • It does not remove the need for supplier contingency planning.
  • It does not replace business continuity ownership.

Enterprise examples

Model provider outage

Primary model API is unavailable.

Normal / desired state:
Workflow degrades to approved alternate or manual queue.
Risk / control concern:
Business process stops completely.

Agent disabled by incident

Agent is killed after breach.

Normal / desired state:
Manual operating procedure takes over.
Risk / control concern:
No one knows how to continue work.
Failure scenarios

No fallback queue

Customers cannot be served when AI is down.

Uncontrolled alternate model

Team switches to unapproved external AI.

Untested recovery

Fallback exists on paper but fails in practice.
