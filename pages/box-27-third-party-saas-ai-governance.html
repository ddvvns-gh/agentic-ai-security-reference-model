Govern native AI capabilities embedded inside enterprise SaaS platforms.

This capability governs AI features delivered by SaaS providers such as copilots, summarisation, AI search and workflow automation. It addresses tenant settings, feature activation, logging, data boundaries and provider obligations.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To ensure third-party SaaS AI features are discovered, assessed, configured, monitored and governed before they expose enterprise data or influence outcomes.

Strategic objective

Enable safe SaaS AI adoption while preventing native provider AI from bypassing internal AI gateways and SDLC controls.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 2 because SaaS AI operates at runtime where users, SaaS data and provider-native AI capabilities intersect.

SaaS AI is a parallel execution path and must not become a governance bypass.
Problem statement

SaaS AI can be enabled by vendors or administrators and may access data already inside platforms such as email, documents, tickets, CRM and collaboration spaces.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Discovers SaaS AI features and usage.
  • Controls enablement, opt-out and tenant configuration.
  • Reviews provider data-use and training settings.
  • Monitors SaaS AI activity and logs.
  • Maintains risk and exception evidence.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not control provider internal model logic.
  • It does not replace third-party risk management.
  • It does not replace internal AI runtime controls.

Enterprise examples

Microsoft 365 Copilot

Copilot summarises SharePoint and Teams content.

Normal / desired state:
Tenant settings, labels and permissions are reviewed.
Risk / control concern:
Copilot surfaces sensitive material across weak permissions.

ServiceNow AI

AI summarises incident records.

Normal / desired state:
Sensitive categories are restricted and logged.
Risk / control concern:
Security investigation details are exposed to broad users.
Failure scenarios

Silent feature enablement

Provider enables a new AI feature by default.

Permission amplification

AI makes hard-to-find sensitive data easy to discover.

Logging gap

AI interactions cannot be reconstructed after incident.
