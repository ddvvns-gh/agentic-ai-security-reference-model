Pause high-impact actions until accountable humans approve or reject them.

This capability handles material actions where the correct response is not automatic termination but escalation for human accountability, dual control or executive approval.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To prevent high-impact AI decisions from proceeding without appropriate human oversight.

Strategic objective

Preserve human accountability for material, regulated, customer-impacting or irreversible AI actions.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This is part of the Decision Routing Gateway because it routes material actions from runtime execution into human accountability and evidence layers.

Agents may recommend, but material commitments require accountable authority.
Problem statement

Some AI actions are technically allowed but still too important to proceed without accountable review.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Pauses high-impact actions before commitment.
  • Routes to accountable owner or dual-control workflow.
  • Presents evidence, rationale and context for review.
  • Records approval, rejection or override.
  • Feeds decision lineage.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not make humans infallible.
  • It does not replace policy evaluation.
  • It does not apply to low-risk routine actions unless configured.

Enterprise examples

Customer compensation

Agent recommends compensation.

Normal / desired state:
Action pauses for authorised approval.
Risk / control concern:
Agent sends offer directly.

Production deployment

Engineering agent wants to deploy change.

Normal / desired state:
Change approval is required.
Risk / control concern:
Agent triggers deployment via alternate tool.
Failure scenarios

Approval fatigue

Too many low-value escalations reduce review quality.

Missing context

Approver cannot assess risk.

Bypass channel

Agent uses email instead of workflow to commit action.
