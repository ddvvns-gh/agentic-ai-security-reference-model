Prevent AI outputs from becoming business commitments without authority, validation and evidence.

Output Commitment Control governs the transition from AI-generated output to real business consequence: draft, recommendation, message, change, transaction or binding decision.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To control when AI-generated outputs become authoritative, persistent, externally visible or operationally binding.

Strategic objective

Enable safe autonomy by separating generation from commitment and applying policy, approval and evidence before material action.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 2 because the risk materialises at runtime when an output is about to leave the model and become useful, visible, actionable or binding.

AI may generate a proposal, but the enterprise decides when that proposal becomes an authorised commitment.
Problem statement

AI-generated outputs can be plausible but wrong. The same content may be harmless as a draft but high risk if sent to a customer, executed as code, filed with a regulator or committed as a transaction.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Classifies output type and intended use.
  • Determines commitment level and impact.
  • Routes outputs to allow, review, approve, reject or quarantine paths.
  • Prevents agents from self-authorising high-impact actions.
  • Records evidence for material commitments.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not guarantee output correctness.
  • It does not replace legal or compliance review where required.
  • It does not replace identity, data or tool controls.

Outcomes delivered

Controlled autonomy

AI can operate faster while staying inside action boundaries.

Clear accountability

Material commitments link to authorised humans and evidence.

Regulatory defensibility

AI-assisted commitments can be reviewed and defended.

Enterprise examples

Customer service agent

Agent drafts customer complaint responses.

Normal / desired state:
Generic guidance may proceed; compensation requires approval.
Risk / control concern:
Agent offers compensation beyond authority.

Engineering agent

Agent proposes code changes.

Normal / desired state:
Draft pull request is allowed.
Risk / control concern:
Agent deploys to production without change approval.
Failure scenarios

Unauthorised compensation

AI offers customer compensation above approved limits.

Unsafe code commit

AI commits generated code directly to protected branch.

Regulatory submission error

AI sends regulator response without compliance approval.
