Prevent AI outputs from becoming commitments without independent validation and authority.

This capability controls the transition from AI output to business consequence. It now explicitly includes asymmetric validation: high-consequence outputs are independently verified by policy-as-code and, where semantic judgement is required, constrained evaluators before human or automated commitment.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To ensure AI-generated outputs remain proposals until policy, authority, validation and evidence allow commitment.

Strategic objective

Separate generation from commitment so AI can assist without self-authorising business impact.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 2 because output risk materialises at runtime when a response, file, API request or message is about to become binding or externally visible.

The model may propose; external controls decide whether the enterprise commits.
Problem statement

An output may be harmless as a draft but dangerous as a customer message, payment instruction, production change or regulatory submission. Relying on the model to approve its own output is a self-policing flaw.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Classifies output type, materiality and reversibility.
  • Performs independent validation before commitment.
  • Routes high-impact outputs to policy engine, constrained evaluator and/or human approval.
  • Prevents agent self-approval of material outcomes.
  • Records commitment evidence and final payload.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not rely on agent confidence as sufficient approval.
  • It does not guarantee output correctness.
  • It does not replace legal, compliance or business review where required.

Enterprise examples

Customer compensation

Agent drafts compensation offer.

Normal / desired state:
Policy checks amount, authority and customer impact before approval.
Risk / control concern:
Agent sends offer because it judged it reasonable.

Regulatory response

Agent drafts regulator submission.

Normal / desired state:
Compliance approval and evidence gate required before send.
Risk / control concern:
Agent submits directly after self-review.
Failure scenarios

Self-approved output

Agent generates and approves the same response.

External message leakage

Customer email includes sensitive data.

Irreversible transaction

Payment is triggered without commitment gate.
