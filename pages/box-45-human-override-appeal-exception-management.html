Human Override, Appeal & Exception Management

The recourse capability: customer-impacting decision appeal, accountable human override, and time-bound exceptions with compensating controls and enforced expiry — so automated decisions remain contestable and exceptions never become permanent.

Purpose and strategic objectives

Purpose

Guarantee a governed recourse pathway for material automated decisions and ensure every exception is owned, compensated and time-bound.

Strategic objective

Convert the capability into a governable, measurable and assurable component of the Agentic AI Security & Governance Reference Model.

Architectural placement

Why this capability sits here

This L3 capability sits at the human-accountability boundary: it is where people contest, override or temporarily except an automated decision, producing evidence that accountability and assurance capabilities consume.

Regulated decisions need recourse, and exceptions can become permanent. This capability binds override to authority and every exception to an owner, compensating control and expiry.

Enterprise scenarios

Customer appeal

A customer contests an automated decision.

Desired state:
Reviewed via recourse path with evidence.
Concern:
No way to contest.

Emergency override

An operator must override a decision.

Desired state:
Authority validated and recorded.
Concern:
Untracked override.

Temporary exception

A team needs a short-term deviation.

Desired state:
Owner, compensating control, expiry set.
Concern:
Exception never expires.
