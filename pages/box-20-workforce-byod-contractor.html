Govern AI access from unmanaged devices and non-employee users.

BYOD and contractor access creates risk because endpoint controls are limited. This capability relies on conditional access, browser isolation, ZTNA and session restrictions to allow productive access without trusting the device.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To permit AI access from unmanaged devices only through constrained and monitored channels.

Strategic objective

Enable flexible work and third-party collaboration without uncontrolled data exposure.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 0 because BYOD and contractor users are externalised human access paths into enterprise AI and SaaS ecosystems.

When the device is not trusted, the session must become the control point.
Problem statement

Unmanaged devices may lack EDR, DLP, patching and enterprise browser controls, yet still access sensitive AI services or SaaS AI features.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Applies conditional access and device posture checks.
  • Uses browser isolation or restricted sessions.
  • Limits downloads, uploads, copy/paste and persistence.
  • Separates contractor and employee access policies.
  • Produces access and session evidence.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not make unmanaged devices trusted.
  • It does not replace contractual controls for contractors.
  • It does not eliminate all screen capture or out-of-band leakage risks.

Outcomes delivered

Safe flexible access

Unmanaged users can work without broad device trust.

Reduced third-party exposure

Contractor access is scoped and monitored.

Session-level governance

Controls follow the session, not just the device.

Enterprise examples

Contractor reviews project data

A contractor uses an AI summariser in a restricted session.

Normal / desired state:
No download or paste to external AI is allowed.
Risk / control concern:
Sensitive documents persist on contractor laptop.

BYOD employee accesses SaaS AI

Employee uses personal device while travelling.

Normal / desired state:
Browser isolation and conditional access apply.
Risk / control concern:
Native SaaS AI surfaces data without session restrictions.
Failure scenarios

Data left on personal device

AI-generated sensitive content is downloaded locally.

Overprivileged contractor

Contractor AI session accesses unnecessary repositories.

No session monitoring

Unmanaged AI activity lacks audit trail.
