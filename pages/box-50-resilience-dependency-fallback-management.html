Resilience Dependency & Fallback Management

Defines how the agentic estate behaves when a dependency fails: fail-open, fail-closed, degrade, queue, manual takeover, or approved model/provider fallback — mapped per control so failure is a governed decision, not an accident.

Purpose and strategic objectives

Purpose

Ensure every critical AI dependency has a defined, evidenced failure mode and a safe, approved fallback path.

Strategic objective

Convert the capability into a governable, measurable and assurable component of the Agentic AI Security & Governance Reference Model.

Architectural placement

Why this capability sits here

This L4 capability sits in resilience operations: it governs degraded and failed states across the gateway, policy engine, model providers, DLP and observability so that control failure does not silently become control absence.

Failure of the gateway, policy engine, model provider, DLP or observability must resolve to a defined, safe state — never an unconsidered fail-open.

Enterprise scenarios

Provider outage

A model provider becomes unavailable.

Desired state:
Approved fallback or safe degrade.
Concern:
Silent fail-open.

Policy engine down

The policy engine is unreachable.

Desired state:
Fail-closed per defined mode.
Concern:
Unevaluated actions proceed.

Critical process

An AI-dependent process must continue.

Desired state:
Manual takeover available.
Concern:
No fallback exists.
