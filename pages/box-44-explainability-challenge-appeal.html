Provide understandable explanations and paths to challenge AI-assisted outcomes.

This capability ensures impacted users, staff, reviewers and auditors can understand material AI-assisted outcomes at the right level and challenge them through a defined process.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To make AI-assisted decisions explainable, reviewable and contestable where impact warrants it.

Strategic objective

Support fairness, customer trust, regulatory defensibility and internal accountability for AI-assisted decisions.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 3 because it turns decision evidence into human-understandable explanation and review pathways.

Evidence is not enough; affected people need understandable reasons and a way to challenge outcomes.
Problem statement

AI decisions may affect customers, employees or operations. If explanations are unavailable or too technical, decisions become difficult to defend.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Generates role-appropriate explanations.
  • Defines challenge and appeal pathways.
  • Links explanations to decision evidence.
  • Supports reviewer and customer-facing summaries.
  • Tracks appeal outcomes and systemic issues.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not require disclosure of sensitive internals or protected IP.
  • It does not guarantee every AI decision is explainable at full model level.
  • It does not replace human review for high-impact outcomes.

Enterprise examples

Customer outcome

AI contributes to service decision.

Normal / desired state:
Customer receives understandable reason and review path.
Risk / control concern:
Customer cannot determine why decision occurred.

Employee workflow decision

AI prioritises internal cases.

Normal / desired state:
Staff can see factors and escalate concern.
Risk / control concern:
Unclear rationale hides unfair treatment.
Failure scenarios

Opaque denial

Customer receives AI-assisted refusal without reason.

No appeal route

Reviewer cannot challenge AI outcome.

Bad explanation

Explanation cites factors not actually used.
