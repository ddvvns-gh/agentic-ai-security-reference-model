Assess legal, intellectual-property, data-use and provider risks before AI adoption.

This capability ensures AI components and services are not adopted solely on technical merit. It addresses provider terms, data-use restrictions, training rights, IP exposure, open-source licences and jurisdictional obligations.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To ensure third-party AI usage is legally permitted, contractually understood and aligned with data, privacy and regulatory obligations.

Strategic objective

Prevent legal, IP, privacy, sovereignty and contract breaches arising from external AI models, SaaS AI features, datasets, tools and providers.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

It sits in Layer -3 because legal and third-party viability must be known before the enterprise depends on a component or service.

An AI component can be technically strong but legally unusable or commercially unacceptable.
Problem statement

AI adoption often involves external models, APIs, datasets, SaaS copilots and open-source components. These can create hidden obligations around training, retention, ownership, output rights and cross-border processing.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Reviews provider terms and data-use policies.
  • Assesses IP, licence and output ownership risk.
  • Supports approved-provider onboarding.
  • Identifies data residency and privacy constraints.
  • Maintains legal and third-party risk evidence.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not perform technical security testing.
  • It does not replace procurement or legal ownership.
  • It does not guarantee provider behaviour beyond agreed terms and evidence.

Outcomes delivered

AI adoption is supported by reviewed terms and documented obligations.

Provider dependencies are visible and risk-assessed.

IP clarity

Teams understand usage rights, restrictions and output ownership.

Enterprise examples

External LLM API

A team wants to use an external model API for customer data processing.

Normal / desired state:
Provider terms are reviewed for retention, training and jurisdiction.
Risk / control concern:
Prompts containing customer data are retained or used for training.

Open-source model

A model is attractive for cost and performance reasons.

Normal / desired state:
Licence and usage restrictions are reviewed before approval.
Risk / control concern:
The licence conflicts with commercial deployment or output usage.
Failure scenarios

Provider changes terms

A SaaS provider modifies AI data-use terms without enterprise review.

Unclear output rights

AI-generated output is used commercially without understanding IP rights.

Jurisdictional breach

Data is processed in an unapproved region through a third-party AI service.
