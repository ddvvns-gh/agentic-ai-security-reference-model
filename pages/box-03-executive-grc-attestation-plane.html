Provide executive and risk-committee visibility over AI risk, control posture, exceptions and accountability.

This capability turns the AI workforce into a governable risk portfolio. It supports executive oversight, risk acceptance, regulatory mapping, control posture reporting and exception management for material AI capabilities.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To provide a formal governance, risk and compliance plane for AI agents, ensuring that material AI risks are visible, owned, accepted and reported through appropriate enterprise forums.

Strategic objective

Make agentic AI adoption defensible to executives, risk committees, auditors and regulators by connecting AI capabilities to risk appetite, control evidence and accountability.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This capability sits in Layer -4 because it governs enterprise accountability and risk posture before and above technical enforcement. It defines how AI risk is escalated, accepted and reported.

Security controls reduce risk, but GRC determines whether remaining risk is acceptable to the enterprise.
Problem statement

AI risk can be fragmented across technology, product, data, security, legal and operational teams. Without an executive GRC plane, risk decisions are inconsistent, exceptions become invisible and regulatory evidence is difficult to assemble.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Maps AI capabilities to risk categories, control objectives and regulatory obligations.
  • Maintains risk exceptions, compensating controls and expiry dates.
  • Supports executive attestations and risk acceptance.
  • Produces board, risk committee and audit reporting.
  • Aggregates control posture, incidents, material risks and remediation status.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not replace detailed technical control operation.
  • It does not automatically make an AI system compliant.
  • It does not remove the need for business ownership and data ownership.
  • It does not replace independent audit or assurance.

Outcomes delivered

Leadership can see material AI risk, control gaps and exceptions.

Regulatory defensibility

Evidence is organised around risk, controls and accountability.

Consistent risk acceptance

Residual risk is accepted by the right accountable owner.

Enterprise examples

High-risk customer agent

A customer-facing agent is proposed for complaints handling.

Normal / desired state:
The agent is risk-tiered, approved and subject to executive attestation.
Risk / control concern:
The agent operates without visibility to risk committee or customer-impact review.

Temporary policy exception

A business asks to launch with incomplete telemetry integration.

Normal / desired state:
Exception is documented with expiry and compensating monitoring.
Risk / control concern:
Exception becomes permanent and control posture degrades.
Failure scenarios

No accountable risk owner

A material AI agent creates customer impact but no executive has accepted the residual risk.

Stale exception register

Policy exceptions remain open beyond agreed expiry.

Audit evidence scramble

Control evidence is assembled manually after an audit request because no GRC evidence model exists.
