Policy-as-Code Governance

Make governance policies machine-enforceable, version-controlled, testable and continuously validated — material requirements compiled, tested, approved and enforced before agent deployment or runtime execution.

Purpose and strategic objectives

Purpose

Convert governance requirements into executable, tested and enforced policy so paper controls become runtime controls.

Strategic objective

Convert the capability into a governable, measurable and assurable component of the Agentic AI Security & Governance Reference Model.

Architectural placement

Why this capability sits here

This L-1 capability is the policy compilation and enforcement backbone, feeding the policy engine, release gate and assurance capabilities.

All material AI governance requirements must be represented as executable policy artifacts. Policies must be compiled, tested, approved and enforced before agent deployment or runtime execution.

Enterprise scenarios

New control

A requirement is defined.

Desired state:
Authored as tested policy code.
Concern:
Paper-only control.

Version drift

Policy is updated.

Desired state:
Runtime binds the new version.
Concern:
Stale enforcement.

Deploy

An agent is promoted.

Desired state:
Blocked without policy.
Concern:
Ungoverned deploy.

Regulatory relevance

  • APRA CPS 230: operational risk, resilience and accountability.
  • APRA CPS 234: control design and operating effectiveness evidence.

  • ISO/IEC 42001: AI management system lifecycle and governance.
  • OWASP LLM / Agentic AI risks.
  • MITRE ATLAS: operational AI attack and response scenarios.
