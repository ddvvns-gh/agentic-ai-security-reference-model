Continuously monitor whether AI behaviour and controls remain acceptable over time.

This capability measures model drift, output quality, bias indicators, control performance, evaluator accuracy and policy effectiveness. It ensures controls remain useful after deployment.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To detect behavioural degradation, bias, unfairness and control weakening after AI systems go live.

Strategic objective

Maintain trust and compliance by proving that AI systems and controls remain effective over time.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 4 because AI behaviour and control effectiveness must be monitored continuously after release.

AI risk changes after deployment; assurance must be continuous.
Problem statement

Models, prompts, data, users and adversaries change. Controls that were effective at launch may degrade, become noisy, miss new patterns or create unintended bias.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Monitors model and output drift.
  • Measures bias, fairness and quality indicators where relevant.
  • Tracks control false positives and false negatives.
  • Assesses evaluator and policy effectiveness.
  • Triggers review, tuning or remediation.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not define fairness policy by itself.
  • It does not replace human judgement or legal review.
  • It does not guarantee complete bias elimination.

v18 hardening — Memory Hygiene Monitoring

Control effectiveness monitoring now includes memory hygiene: TTL compliance, epoch violations, stale memory reuse, poisoning detections, quarantine rates and recall of low-trust memory. This links runtime memory governance to continuous assurance.
