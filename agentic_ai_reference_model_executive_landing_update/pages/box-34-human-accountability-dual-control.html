Anchor material AI decisions to accountable humans and segregation of duties.

This capability ensures humans remain accountable for high-impact, regulated or irreversible actions. It defines approval authority, dual control, override rules and segregation of duties for AI-assisted decisions.

Purpose and strategic objective

Purpose

To maintain human accountability and control over material AI-driven outcomes.

Strategic objective

Prevent agents from self-authorising critical business actions while supporting safe AI-assisted decision-making.

Architectural placement

Why this sits here

This sits in Layer 3 because it governs human accountability after runtime controls identify a material decision point.

Accountability cannot be delegated to a model; it must remain anchored to authorised roles.
Problem statement

AI systems can recommend actions, but enterprises and regulators expect accountable humans to own material outcomes.

Capability description

What the capability does

  • Defines accountable roles and approval authority.
  • Enforces four-eyes or dual-control review.
  • Supports override, rejection and appeal paths.
  • Records approver identity and rationale.
  • Maintains segregation of duties.

What the capability does not do

  • It does not require human approval for every low-risk action.
  • It does not replace technical prevention controls.
  • It does not eliminate the need for training and role design.

Enterprise examples

Payment instruction

Agent prepares a payment.

Normal / desired state:
Dual approvers approve before execution.
Risk / control concern:
Agent initiates payment with one click.

Customer hardship decision

Agent recommends customer outcome.

Normal / desired state:
Accountable officer reviews rationale.
Risk / control concern:
Decision is applied without human owner.
Failure scenarios

No approver

Material action cannot be traced to accountable person.

Approval collusion

Dual approval is not independent.

No rationale

Approval exists but cannot be defended.
