Executive introduction

Trust-centric governance for enterprise agentic AI

Agentic AI systems can reason, delegate work, invoke tools, create artefacts, use memory and make commitments. Traditional security and governance models were not designed for this operating model. This reference model provides a framework for establishing trust through governance, controls, evidence and assurance.

Why this matters

AI is becoming autonomous

Agents no longer simply answer questions. They can plan, delegate, invoke tools, interact with other agents and influence business outcomes.

Traditional controls are not enough

Applications execute predefined logic. Agents reason over goals and context. That creates new trust, identity, provenance and assurance challenges.

Trust must be assured

The model does not ask the enterprise to simply trust AI. It establishes confidence through controls, evidence and assurance.

The challenge is no longer only securing applications. The challenge is establishing confidence in outcomes produced by dynamic, delegated and partially autonomous systems.
The Trust Chain

Artefact Trust

Can we trust the models, prompts, datasets, agents, tools, MCP servers, memory stores and AI-generated artefacts used by the system?

Identity Trust

Can we trust the human, agent, child-agent and workload identities acting within the system and the authority delegated to them?

Execution Trust

Can we trust the runtime environment, control plane, sandbox, tool gateway and execution pathway through which actions occur?

Decision Trust

Can we validate, explain and reconstruct the outputs, commitments and material decisions produced by the system?

The operating model

Build Trust

Governance, provenance, ownership, architecture, policy and identity foundations are established before runtime.

Execute Trust

Agents, tools, memory, sandboxes and runtime controls operate within externally enforced boundaries.

Assure Trust

Telemetry, lineage, metrics, evidence and assurance demonstrate that the system remains trustworthy.

What makes this model different?

AI Provenance & Supply Chain Assurance

Trust starts with artefacts. Origin, ownership, dependencies, approvals and attestations become foundational.

Agentic Identity Mesh

Trust follows delegated authority from humans to agents, child agents, tools and systems.

Externalized Control Planes

Agents reason. External controls enforce. LLMs are not deterministic security boundaries.

Semantic Blast-Radius Isolation

Natural language can carry instructions, contamination and intent. Agent-to-agent payloads require boundaries.

State-Locked Validation

High-impact actions must execute against the same state snapshot that was validated and approved.

Decision Lineage

The goal is not just logging. The goal is reconstructability: what happened, why, under what authority and with what evidence.
