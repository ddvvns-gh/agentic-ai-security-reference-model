E-OCC-002

Approval Record

The record of a human approval decision on an above-threshold commitment — approver, validated authority, rationale and outcome.

Evidence IDE-OCC-002
Produced byC-OCC-004
FormatStructured event (JSON)
IntegritySigned / hash-chained
Retention7 yr (financial)
ConsumersAudit · Risk
Purpose
Proves that a material commitment received valid human approval from someone whose delegated authority covered it. It is the primary defence against both repudiation and rubber-stamping, and the segregation-of-duties evidence an auditor expects.
Required fields
FieldDescription
record_idUnique immutable identifier for this approval event.
timestampUTC time of the approval decision.
commitment_idCorrelation key to the commitment and its lineage (E-OCC-004).
approver_identityAuthenticated identity of the approving human.
approver_authority_refThe delegated-authority entry validated for this approver.
decision / rationaleApprove or reject, with the recorded reason.
dual_auth_refSecond-approver reference where dual authorisation applied.
threshold_refLink to the threshold evaluation (E-OCC-007) that required approval.
signatureCryptographic signature linking the record into the tamper-evident chain.
Collection & retention

Collection

  • Emitted by the approval workflow at the moment of decision.
  • Shipped via structured telemetry (Box 46) to the evidence store.

Retention & disposal

  • Retain per records policy; financial/regulatory typically 7 years.
  • Immutable for the retention period; disposed under records governance (Box 11).
Integrity
Records are signed and hash-chained so deletion or alteration is detectable — required for the record to be relied on in dispute or examination.
Assurance use
An auditor samples above-threshold commitments and confirms each carries a valid E-OCC-002 from an authorised approver with a genuine rationale — direct CPS 234 and segregation-of-duties evidence for C-OCC-004.
